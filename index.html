FAQs

Get the answers to common questions about St. Paul’s GitHub on Campus.

GitHub on Campus is a student-led community at St. Paul’s University that helps students learn, collaborate, and contribute to open source projects. We focus on areas like web development, mobile development, cloud computing, AI, and more!

You can join by attending our workshops, events, and project meetups. Membership is open to all St. Paul’s students. Join our WhatsApp group and follow our GitHub Organization to see ongoing projects you can contribute to!

Students can work on a wide range of projects including websites, mobile apps, technical documentation, UI/UX design, and open-source tools. You’ll get hands-on experience collaborating through GitHub!

We organize a variety of activities, including hands-on coding workshops, open-source sprints, hackathons, tech talks, and collaborative projects. We also partner with other tech communities to host special events that help students stay updated with the latest trends in software development.

No, our events are designed for all skill levels — from complete beginners to experienced developers. Whether you’re just starting your coding journey or looking to contribute to advanced projects, there’s a place for you at GitHub on Campus!
